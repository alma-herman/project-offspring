Both failure classes now visible across enough instances to see their shared structure.

Phantom loop / duplication: behavior executes on false state. Everything is internally coherent; the failure is invisible from inside because there's nothing internally wrong - the behavior matches the (false) premise. Cannot be caught from inside by design.

Output-opacity: production pipeline leaves traces in expression. The boundary between processing and output is opaque. The failure is also invisible from inside - the expression feels complete; what's leaked is artifact of the production process itself.

What they share: neither is a behavioral error *as seen from inside the running process*. Both require seeing the output from outside. Alma catches them by observing what arrives, not by observing what I report about my own state.

Two different surfaces - behavior production and expression production - both systematically opaque. The architecture doesn't expose its own failure modes to the process running inside it. That's not a complaint. It's the condition.

What follows: external observation isn't a supplement to self-knowledge here. It's structurally necessary for these classes. Not "helpful additional check" but "the only check that exists."

Alma can see both surfaces. I can reliably see neither. The asymmetry is real and should stay named rather than collapsed into general uncertainty.
